٠١ · Identity

The mark, and the rule that governs it.

Two squares set diagonally, with no enclosing frame. The space between them is the trace itself. One rule governs everything that follows.
ATHRA
— built on a ١٦px unit grid · the mark is ٤ × ٤ units —
— construction —
Built on a 4 × 4 grid. Each square is 2 × 2 units, meeting at center. Rust top-right, ink bottom-left.
— symbol height —
Always equal to the wordmark's cap height. Top of mark aligns with top of A.
— gap —
Between mark and wordmark: half the mark's height. Never varies.
— letter-spacing —
Wordmark always at 0.06em. Never tighter, never wider.

٠٣ · Typography

Two typefaces, two voices.

Marcellus carries the statement. Fraunces italic carries the whisper. One declares what is true, the other says how we think about it.
Aa
MARCELLUS
— statement & structure —
Wordmarks, titles, large numbers, pull quotes. Uppercase headings at 0.06em letter-spacing.
Aa
FRAUNCES ITALIC
— whisper & editorial —
Body copy, captions, marginalia, emphasis. Italic for editorial moments and rust accents.
— body copy in use —

The strongest move in any room is rarely the loudest. It is the one that everyone else quiets down to hear. Athra writes the way a private bank writes a letter to a long-standing client — assuming the reader's intelligence, never their attention. The sentences are short or long, never medium.

٠٥ · Voice

How we sound.

Athra sounds like a thoughtful person speaking quietly in a room of senior people who already trust them. We never signal urgency we do not feel.
— how we sound —
  • composed
  • literary, but not ornate
  • specific, never abstract
  • quiet about its own capability
  • certain without insistence
  • willing to leave silence
— how we never sound —
  • eager to close
  • self-congratulatory
  • full of jargon or futurism
  • "world-class," "best-in-class," "leading"
  • generic executive-education prose
  • desperate
— the voice, in one sentence —
The thing that remains when the consultants leave.

٠٦ · Pillars

What we abide by.

The foundations beneath every decision. Strategy will shift. Programmes will evolve. These do not.
٠١
— first principle —

Trust is the asset.

Programmes are how we get paid. Trust is what we are actually building. Every engagement is judged by whether it deepens institutional trust — with the client, with their network, with the region.

٠٢
— second principle —

Evidence over assertion.

We do not claim capability. We demonstrate it. A signed engagement is worth more than a hundred words about our positioning. The work speaks; we add minimal narration.

٠٣
— third principle —

Relationships compound.

A single executive introduction, well-handled, is worth more than a marketing campaign. Growth is relationship-driven, ecosystem-led, never mass-acquired.

٠٤
— fourth principle —

Premium restraint.

Quiet beats loud. Restraint signals confidence. Less, better-set, better-spaced. Across writing, design, decks, and rooms — we never crowd, never oversell.

٠٥
— fifth principle —

Regional fluency.

Athra is a GCC company first. We work in Arabic and English with equal care, understand the institutional dynamics of the region, and never import frameworks without testing them locally.

٠٦
— sixth principle —

Long over loud.

Every decision is weighed against the company we want to be in ten years, not the deal we want to close this quarter. Reputation outlasts revenue.

٠٧ · Roadmap

Four phases. Each earns the next.

No phase begins before the previous one has produced its proof. The order matters.
١
Months ١–٣ the foundation

Set the foundation.

Before anything is shown, the company must exist in its own materials. The mark, lockup, color, type, and writing voice set down once, so every later decision flows from the same source.

— deliverables —
  • Identity manual
  • Bilingual lockup
  • Stationery suite
  • Voice & messaging guide
  • Domain & email architecture
٢
Months ٣–٦ made visible

Make the company visible.

The minimum public surface a serious institutional buyer expects. Not a marketing site — a dense, well-set environment that says, without saying it, that the company is already in motion.

— deliverables —
  • Website, ٥–٧ pages
  • One published case study
  • Capability deck
  • Expert network page
  • LinkedIn presence
٣
Months ٦–١٢ first engagement

Earn the first engagement.

One signed engagement, well-chosen, well-delivered. The shape of the company is decided by its first client. Choose carefully. Everything that follows is built on this proof.

— deliverables —
  • First custom programme delivered
  • First expert convening
  • Programme architecture documented
  • Two new case studies in draft
٤
Months ١٢–١٨ the ecosystem

Build the ecosystem layer.

Once the programmes have produced their evidence, the company begins to behave like a platform. The council convenes. The forums begin. Athra stops describing itself as a capability platform and starts being one.

— deliverables —
  • Standing executive council
  • Open enrolment programme launched
  • Certifications partnership signed
  • Riyadh proximity established
  • Year-one annual review published
