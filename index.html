01 · Identity

The mark, and the rule that governs it.

Two squares set diagonally, with no enclosing frame. The space between them is the trace itself — the offset, the imprint, the suggestion of what passed through.
— the mark, in primary form —
— construction —
Built on a 4 × 4 grid. Each square is 2 × 2 units, meeting at the center point. Rust top-right, ink bottom-left.
— symbol height —
Always equal to the wordmark's cap height. The top of the mark aligns with the top of the A.
— gap —
The space between mark and wordmark is half the mark's height. Always.
— clear space —
One full mark-height on every side. Nothing enters that frame.

02 · Typography

Two typefaces, two voices.

Marcellus carries the statement. Fraunces italic carries the whisper. They never compete because they were chosen for one another — one declares what is true, the other says how we think about it.
Aa
MARCELLUS
— for statement & structure —
Wordmarks, chapter titles, large numbers, pull quotes. Always uppercase for headings, set at 0.06em letter-spacing.
Aa
FRAUNCES ITALIC
— for whisper & editorial —
Body copy, captions, marginalia, emphasis. Variable weight, italic preferred for editorial moments and rust accents.
— body copy in use —

The strongest move in any room is rarely the loudest. It is the one that everyone else quiets down to hear. Athra writes the way a private bank writes a letter to a long-standing client — assuming the reader's intelligence, never their attention. The sentences are short or long, never medium. Short for emphasis. Long when an idea needs space to develop properly.

03 · Color

Four colors, no more.

Restrained on purpose. Parchment is the ground. Ink is the voice. Rust is the accent. Ochre is reserved for dark applications where rust loses contrast. No greys. No blues. No additions.

04 · Voice

How we sound.

Athra sounds like a thoughtful person speaking quietly in a room of senior people who already trust them. We do not pitch. We do not chase. We never signal urgency we do not feel.
— how we sound —
  • composed
  • literary, but not ornate
  • specific, never abstract
  • quiet about its own capability
  • certain without insistence
  • willing to leave silence
— how we never sound —
  • eager to close
  • self-congratulatory
  • full of jargon or futurism
  • "world-class," "best-in-class," "leading"
  • generic executive-education prose
  • desperate
— the voice, in one sentence —
The thing that remains when the consultants leave.
05 · Roadmap

Four phases. Each one earns the next.

No phase begins before the previous one has produced its proof. The order matters. The deliverables are the evidence a phase is complete, not the activities of the phase itself.
```
1
Months 1–3 the foundation

Set the foundation.

Before anything is shown, the company must exist in its own materials. The mark, the wordmark, the bilingual lockup, the color and type systems, and the writing voice all need to be set down — once — so that every later decision flows from the same source.

— deliverables —
  • Identity manual
  • Bilingual lockup
  • Stationery suite
  • Voice & messaging guide
  • Domain & email architecture
2
Months 3–6 made visible

Make the company visible.

The minimum public surface that a serious institutional buyer expects to encounter. Not a marketing site. A small, dense, well-set environment that says — without needing to say it — that the company is real, capable, and already in motion.

— deliverables —
  • Website, 5–7 pages
  • One published case study
  • Capability deck
  • Expert network page
  • LinkedIn presence
3
Months 6–12 first engagement

Earn the first institutional engagement.

One signed engagement, well-chosen, well-delivered. The shape of the company is decided by its first client. Choose carefully. The first engagement is the only piece of evidence that the next ten will be built upon.

— deliverables —
  • First custom programme delivered
  • First expert convening
  • Programme architecture documented
  • Two new case studies in draft
4
Months 12–18 the ecosystem

Build the ecosystem layer.

Once the programmes have produced their evidence, the company begins to behave like a platform. The standing council convenes. The forums begin. This is when Athra stops describing itself as a capability platform and starts being one.

— deliverables —
  • Standing executive council
  • Open enrolment programme launched
  • Certifications partnership signed
  • Riyadh proximity established
  • Year-one annual review published
```
A company is built twice — first as an idea on paper, then as a thing in the world.
These are the guidelines. To be revised when the work demands it, and not before.
